# Pitch Narrative Guide

This guide helps the Biz team convert the NeTrix business plan into a persuasive pitch narrative. The pitch should be clear, disciplined, and grounded in the Direction Memo. It should not over-explain every feature. Its purpose is to make the audience understand why NeTrix matters now, why the first wedge is credible, and why the product loop can become valuable.

## 1. Core Pitch Logic

The pitch should follow a narrative progression:

```text
AI-assisted learning is changing student behaviour.
Academic knowledge is becoming more private and fragmented.
Students need a better way to discover knowledge, experience, and peers.
NeTrix turns academic posts into profiles and profiles into explainable connections.
The first MVP validates this loop with Math, Computer Science, and EEE students at UNNC.
```

This storyline is stronger than a feature list because it explains why the product should exist.

## 2. Recommended Opening

The opening should establish the problem in a way that feels specific to student life. A possible version is:

> Students today have more learning tools than ever, but academic knowledge is not necessarily becoming more shared. Questions disappear in private chats, useful resources circulate unevenly, experience remains inside small friend groups, and AI-assisted learning often stays inside individual black boxes. NeTrix is built to make academic knowledge and relevant peers easier to discover.

This opening connects AI, fragmentation, and peer discovery without making the pitch sound like a generic AI product.

## 3. Product Explanation

The product should be explained through the loop:

> Students create Q&A, Resource, and Experience Sharing posts. These posts help form editable academic profiles. NeTrix then uses AI-assisted recommendation to suggest relevant academic peers and explain why each connection may be useful. Students can request to connect, and messaging opens only after acceptance.

This explanation is concise but complete. It shows the main user journey and makes clear that AI supports connection rather than replacing interaction.

## 4. Positioning Language

The recommended short positioning statement is:

> NeTrix is the AI-assisted academic connection layer for UNNC students.

The recommended longer positioning statement is:

> NeTrix helps students share academic questions, resources, and experience; build user-confirmed academic profiles; and discover relevant peers through explainable AI-assisted recommendations.

The pitch should avoid calling NeTrix only a forum, a resource hub, a social media platform, or an AI chatbot. Those labels are either too narrow or strategically misleading.

## 5. Slide-Level Structure

If the Biz team prepares a pitch deck, the recommended slide structure is:

| Slide | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Title | Introduce NeTrix and the academic connection positioning. |
| Problem | Explain fragmented academic information and private AI-assisted learning. |
| User Pain | Show how the problem appears in UNNC student life. |
| Solution | Present the post-profile-recommendation-connection loop. |
| Product Flow | Show the first MVP journey from feed to connection. |
| Target Wedge | Explain why Math, Computer Science, and EEE are the first users. |
| Differentiation | Compare NeTrix with group chats, school channels, resource folders, LinkedIn, and AI chatbots. |
| Evidence | Present survey insights responsibly as early signals. |
| Validation Plan | Show interviews, seeded-content testing, MVP metrics, and next learning goals. |
| Business Model | Present staged monetisation after adoption validation. |
| Roadmap | Connect the first wedge to UNNC-wide expansion. |
| Ask Or Next Step | State what the team needs next: users, validation, technical build, or support. |

## 6. Evidence Story

The evidence slide should not overstate the existing 60 survey responses. It should present them as early discovery data and then explain how the team will strengthen evidence through targeted interviews and MVP behavioural metrics.

Recommended wording:

> Our initial 60-response exploratory survey gives early evidence of fragmented academic information habits and interest in improved academic discovery. We treat this as a discovery signal, not final proof. The next validation stage will combine targeted interviews with Math, Computer Science, and EEE students and behavioural testing through the MVP.

This wording is intellectually honest and makes the team appear more credible.

## 7. Revenue Story

The revenue story should be staged rather than aggressive. The pitch should emphasise that NeTrix must first validate student adoption and network value. After that, possible revenue paths include institutional partnership, premium academic support tools, and expansion to other campus contexts.

Recommended wording:

> NeTrix will prioritise adoption and academic trust before direct monetisation. If the MVP validates repeated use and network value, the strongest revenue paths are institutional partnerships, premium academic support features, and later adaptation to other university contexts.

## 8. Tone And Style

The pitch should sound academically credible, strategically mature, and student-aware. It should not sound like a generic start-up template. The best tone is confident but not exaggerated: NeTrix has a strong thesis, a clear first wedge, and a disciplined validation plan, but it should not claim that the market has already been proven before MVP evidence exists.

The audience should leave with one central impression: NeTrix is trying to make AI-assisted learning more connected, not more isolated.
