# CVP, USP, And Revenue Model Guide

This document explains how the Biz team should determine NeTrix's customer value proposition, unique selling proposition, and revenue-model hypothesis. These three elements must be connected. The CVP explains why users would care. The USP explains why NeTrix is different. The revenue model explains how value may later be captured without damaging the user network.

## 1. Customer Value Proposition

The customer value proposition should be written for the first real users, not for a vague future market. In the current direction, the first users are UNNC Math, Computer Science, and EEE students who face friction in finding academic questions, resources, experience, and relevant peers.

The CVP should answer four questions:

| Question | NeTrix-Specific Interpretation |
| --- | --- |
| Who is the first user? | UNNC Math, Computer Science, and EEE students who actively engage with academic learning, resources, coursework, projects, or peer support. |
| What is the pain? | Academic knowledge, experience, resources, and potential collaborators are fragmented across private chats, small groups, school channels, and AI black boxes. |
| What outcome does the user want? | Faster discovery of useful academic information, more visibility into peer experience, and easier access to relevant academic connections. |
| Why is NeTrix credible? | It combines post-based academic signals, user-confirmed profiles, explainable AI recommendation, and a verified UNNC context. |

The recommended CVP is:

> NeTrix helps UNNC students discover academic knowledge, experience, and relevant peers more efficiently by turning academic posts into profile signals and using AI-assisted recommendations to support meaningful academic connections.

This statement can be shortened for pitch use:

> NeTrix helps UNNC students find the right academic information and the right academic peers at the right time.

## 2. Unique Selling Proposition

The USP should focus on the combination that competitors or substitutes do not provide. NeTrix should not claim that no one shares resources, asks questions, or uses AI. Those behaviours already exist. The stronger claim is that existing behaviours are fragmented and not connected into a coherent academic network.

| Alternative | What It Provides | What It Does Not Provide |
| --- | --- | --- |
| WeChat groups and private chats | Fast informal communication. | Persistent, searchable, profile-linked academic signals and explainable peer discovery. |
| Official school channels | Formal announcements and institutional information. | Student-generated experience, peer expertise, and social academic matching. |
| Generic resource folders | Stored files and links. | Academic identity, discussion context, recommendations, and connection workflows. |
| Q&A forums | Question and answer exchange. | Campus-specific academic profiles and peer connection recommendations. |
| LinkedIn | Professional identity and career networking. | Student-level academic identity, module-level learning context, and campus study connections. |
| AI chatbots | Private answer generation and tutoring-like support. | Shared academic visibility, peer discovery, and community knowledge circulation. |

The recommended USP is:

> NeTrix is the first verified UNNC academic connection layer that links student-generated academic posts, editable academic profiles, and explainable AI-assisted peer recommendations in one campus-specific network.

This USP should be tested, not assumed. The business plan should present it as the intended differentiation and then propose validation methods to examine whether users perceive it as meaningful.

## 3. Revenue Model Principles

NeTrix should not monetise too early in a way that weakens trust, discourages participation, or makes the platform feel transactional before network value exists. The first business objective is validation of repeated student use. Revenue should be presented as a staged hypothesis.

The strongest monetisation logic is likely to emerge after NeTrix has proven that it can create a dense academic network. Until then, the business plan should separate first-phase validation from later-phase value capture.

## 4. Recommended Revenue Model Hypotheses

The following revenue directions are plausible, but each requires validation.

| Model | Description | Fit With NeTrix | Key Risk |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Freemium student tools | Basic community access is free; advanced personal organisation, profile analytics, or AI assistance could become premium later. | Potentially aligned if premium features enhance personal academic productivity without restricting core community value. | Charging students too early may suppress adoption. |
| Institutional partnership | Schools, faculties, or departments support the platform as academic engagement infrastructure. | Strong long-term fit if NeTrix can demonstrate improved academic communication, peer support, and resource circulation. | Requires institutional trust, data governance, and evidence of educational value. |
| Sponsored academic resources | Carefully selected academic, career, or learning partners promote relevant resources inside the platform. | Possible later if sponsorship remains highly relevant and non-intrusive. | Poorly controlled sponsorship could damage credibility. |
| B2B expansion to other campuses | After UNNC validation, the model could be adapted for other universities or international campuses. | Strong long-term scalability narrative. | Premature unless the first campus use case is validated. |
| Talent and project matching partnerships | Verified academic profiles could support project, research, competition, or internship matching. | Potentially valuable because profile signals are academically meaningful. | Must avoid drifting into generic professional recruitment too early. |

The recommended business-plan position is not to choose a single aggressive revenue model now. The better approach is to define a staged monetisation path:

| Stage | Primary Objective | Revenue Position |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stage 1: MVP validation | Prove that students post, build profiles, and request connections. | No direct monetisation; focus on adoption and evidence. |
| Stage 2: Campus network growth | Expand majors, improve resource density, and strengthen recommendation quality. | Explore institutional support or lightweight premium academic tools. |
| Stage 3: Platform expansion | Move from UNNC validation to repeatable campus model. | Evaluate institutional licensing, campus partnerships, and carefully governed premium services. |

## 5. Recommended Business Model Statement

For the first business-plan draft, use a cautious but credible statement:

> NeTrix will prioritise student adoption and network validation before direct monetisation. In the near term, the platform should remain free for first users in order to build content density and academic trust. If the MVP validates repeated use, the most credible revenue paths are institutional partnerships, premium academic support features, and later expansion to other university contexts.

## 6. Decisions The Biz Team Must Make

The Biz team should decide which revenue hypothesis is most appropriate for the first business-plan version. The recommended default is to position institutional partnership as the strongest long-term revenue direction, while keeping freemium academic tools as a secondary option. This fits the product's infrastructure narrative and avoids making the platform feel like a paid student app before its network value is proven.
