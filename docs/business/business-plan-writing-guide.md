# NeTrix Business Plan Writing Guide

This guide defines the recommended structure and writing logic for the NeTrix business plan. It is intended to help the Biz team produce a coherent, evidence-conscious document rather than a collection of disconnected sections.

The business plan should be written from the strategic baseline in `docs/strategy/direction-memo.md`. The central argument is that NeTrix is not merely a resource-sharing website. It is an AI-assisted academic connection network that helps students discover academic knowledge, experience, and relevant peers inside a verified UNNC context.

## 1. Recommended Business Plan Structure

The business plan should follow a sequence that moves from problem to evidence, then from solution to execution.

| Section | Function | Main Question To Answer |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Executive Summary | Present the whole opportunity in a compressed form. | What is NeTrix, who is it for, why now, and how will it be validated? |
| Problem And Context | Define the academic information gap and why it matters in an AI-assisted learning environment. | What is painful or inefficient for students today? |
| Target Users | Define the first wedge and expansion logic. | Why begin with Math, Computer Science, and EEE students? |
| Solution Overview | Explain the product loop and the role of AI. | How do posts, profiles, recommendations, and connections create value? |
| Customer Value Proposition | State the user-facing value in precise terms. | Why would students choose to use NeTrix repeatedly? |
| Unique Selling Proposition | Distinguish NeTrix from alternatives. | Why is NeTrix different from group chats, school pages, forums, LinkedIn, or AI chatbots? |
| Market And Evidence | Use survey data, interviews, and reasonable market framing. | What evidence suggests that the problem and solution are worth testing? |
| Business Model | Present a staged and realistic monetisation hypothesis. | How could NeTrix capture value after student adoption is validated? |
| Go-To-Market And Validation | Describe how the team will reach, test, and learn from the first users. | How will the team move from idea to validated usage? |
| MVP And Operations | Summarise what the product must prove in its first version. | What must be built and measured before expansion? |
| Risks And Mitigation | Show strategic maturity and credibility. | What could fail, and how will the team manage it? |
| Roadmap | Connect near-term execution to long-term vision. | How does the first wedge lead to a UNNC-wide academic infrastructure? |

## 2. Core Argument

The business plan should be built around a clear causal argument. AI-assisted learning is becoming more common, but much of its output remains private. At the same time, student questions, resources, experience, and peer expertise are already scattered across chats, small social circles, and inconsistent school channels. This creates a new academic information gap: students may have more tools than before, but less shared visibility into the learning activity around them.

NeTrix addresses this gap by making academic signals visible and useful. Students contribute Q&A posts, Resource posts, and Experience Sharing posts. These posts help form academic profiles. The platform then uses AI-assisted recommendations to help students find relevant peers and form intentional academic connections.

The business plan should repeatedly return to this mechanism. The value of NeTrix does not come from any single feature in isolation. It comes from the loop between content, identity, recommendation, and connection.

## 3. Evidence Standard

The existing 60 survey responses should be used as early evidence, not as final proof. The business plan may cite them to illustrate problem awareness, user interest, or early segment patterns, but it should avoid language such as "students prove", "the market confirms", or "the data shows conclusively" unless the sampling method justifies such claims.

Recommended evidence language includes "early survey responses suggest", "preliminary evidence indicates", "initial discovery points toward", and "this requires further validation through targeted interviews and MVP usage data".

The strongest business plan will combine three evidence types: survey data, qualitative interviews, and MVP behavioural signals. Survey data can describe early patterns. Interviews can explain why those patterns exist. MVP usage data can reveal whether students actually behave in the way the product thesis predicts.

## 4. Section-Level Writing Guidance

The Executive Summary should be written last, even though it appears first. It should include the product definition, first target wedge, problem context, solution mechanism, early evidence, business-model hypothesis, and next validation milestone in a concise form.

The Problem section should avoid becoming a list of unrelated inconveniences. The stronger problem is not simply that information is scattered. The deeper problem is that AI-assisted learning and private peer communication can make academic knowledge less visible across the community.

The Solution section should be written around the product loop. Instead of listing features separately, explain how posts produce signals, profiles organise signals, AI recommends relevant peers, and connection requests turn discovery into academic relationships.

The Market section should focus first on the campus context rather than forcing a broad global market estimate too early. A credible first business plan can present UNNC as the initial market, Math / CS / EEE as the validation wedge, and other majors or universities as expansion scenarios.

The Business Model section should be presented as a staged hypothesis. Student adoption and network value must come before aggressive monetisation. Early revenue claims should be conservative and tied to future validation.

## 5. Recommended Business Plan Thesis Statement

The following statement can be used as the conceptual centre of the business plan:

> NeTrix is an AI-assisted academic connection network for UNNC students. It responds to the growing academic information gap created by fragmented campus communication and private AI-assisted learning. By turning academic posts into profile signals and using AI to recommend relevant peers, NeTrix helps students discover knowledge, experience, and academic connections in a verified campus environment.

## 6. Common Writing Errors To Avoid

The business plan should not frame NeTrix as a generic resource platform, because that makes it too easy to compare with existing resource folders, school websites, or group chats. It should not frame NeTrix as an AI chatbot, because that contradicts the human-connection thesis. It should not overstate the existing survey data, because a small early sample cannot prove whole-population demand without stronger sampling conditions. It should not define the market so broadly that the first MVP appears disconnected from the execution capacity of the team.

The strongest version of the business plan will be specific, staged, and intellectually honest. It should show that the team understands both the size of the long-term vision and the narrowness required for the first validation cycle.
